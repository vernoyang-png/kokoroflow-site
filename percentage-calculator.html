Percentage formulas and examples

Find a percentage of a number

Convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply: result = percentage ÷ 100 × number. For example, 20% of 150 is 0.20 × 150 = 30. This is useful for discounts, tips, commissions, and budget allocations.

Find what percent one value is of another

Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100: percentage = part ÷ whole × 100. If 45 people out of 180 completed a task, the completion share is 45 ÷ 180 × 100 = 25%.

Calculate percentage change

Subtract the old value from the new value, divide by the old value, then multiply by 100: change = (new − old) ÷ old × 100. Moving from 80 to 100 is a 25% increase. Moving from 100 to 80 is a 20% decrease because the starting value is different.

Common mistakes

  • Percentage points and percent change are not the same. A rate moving from 10% to 12% rises by 2 percentage points but increases by 20% relative to its starting rate.
  • A decrease followed by an equal percentage increase does not return to the original value.
  • Percentage change is undefined when the starting value is zero.
