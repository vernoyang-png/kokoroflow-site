How sales tax works

Sales tax in the United States depends on state, county, and city rules. This calculator helps estimate a final price when you already know the tax rate.

Formula

Sales tax = price × tax rate. Final total = price + sales tax.

Use your local rate from a receipt, store checkout page, or official local source for the best estimate.

How sales tax estimates work

The formula

Tax amount = pre-tax price × tax rate. The final price is the pre-tax price plus the tax amount. A $75 purchase at an 8.25% rate produces an estimated $6.19 tax and an $81.19 total after rounding to cents.

Why the checkout total may differ

Sales tax rules depend on the buyer's location, the seller's obligations, and the product or service. State, county, city, and special-district rates may be combined. Some groceries, medicines, digital products, clothing, or services may be exempt or taxed differently. Marketplace and delivery fees can also change the taxable amount.

Choosing the correct rate

Use the rate that applies to the transaction location, not a remembered statewide headline rate. For an exact filing or business decision, confirm the current rate with the relevant state or local tax authority. This calculator does not look up jurisdictions and does not determine whether an item is taxable.

Frequently asked questions

Can I work backward from a total? Divide the tax-inclusive total by one plus the rate expressed as a decimal. Why is there a one-cent difference? Merchants may round tax per item or on the whole order. Is this tax advice? No. It is a general arithmetic estimator.
