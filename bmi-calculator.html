What BMI means

Body Mass Index is a simple estimate based on height and weight. It can be useful as a broad screening number, but it does not measure body composition, muscle, age, or health conditions.

US BMI formula

BMI = weight in pounds ÷ height in inches squared × 703.

For personal medical questions, use a qualified health professional.

How to interpret a BMI result

The formula

For US measurements, BMI equals weight in pounds divided by height in inches squared, multiplied by 703. For metric measurements, it is kilograms divided by meters squared. The calculator converts feet and inches into total inches before applying the formula.

What BMI can and cannot tell you

BMI is a screening measure based only on height and weight. It does not directly measure body fat, muscle mass, bone density, body composition, fitness, or health. Athletes, older adults, pregnant people, and people with different body builds may receive results that do not reflect their individual health status.

Adult screening ranges

Common adult reference categories describe values below 18.5 as underweight, 18.5–24.9 as healthy weight, 25.0–29.9 as overweight, and 30 or above as obesity. These categories should be interpreted with other information by a qualified professional. Children and teenagers require age- and sex-specific growth charts rather than adult thresholds.

Frequently asked questions

Is BMI a diagnosis? No. Should I change medication or diet because of this number? Consult a qualified healthcare professional first. Why does rounding change the result? Small measurement differences can shift a value near a category boundary.
