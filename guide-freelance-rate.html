Freelance planning guide

Set a freelance rate from capacity, not wishful hours

A sustainable starting rate covers personal income, business costs, non-billable time, and uncertainty.

Reviewed: July 2026 · 6 minute read

Start with the revenue requirement

Add desired personal income and annual business costs. Business costs can include software, equipment, insurance, accounting, training, marketing, workspace, platform fees, and payment charges. Then add a contingency or profit margin for slower periods and reinvestment.

Estimate billable capacity honestly

A 40-hour working week rarely contains 40 billable hours. Proposals, administration, lead generation, client communication, bookkeeping, and professional development still consume time. Multiply realistic billable hours by realistic working weeks—not by every hour in the calendar.

Baseline rate = annual revenue requirement ÷ annual billable hours.

Hourly rate versus project price

The calculated hourly rate is a floor for planning. A project price should also reflect scope, complexity, urgency, responsibility, revisions, meetings, usage rights, and the value of the outcome. Estimate the work internally, add risk, and describe deliverables clearly.

Example

A freelancer wants 60,000 of personal income, expects 12,000 of business costs, adds a 15% margin, and can bill 25 hours across 46 weeks. The revenue target is 82,800 and billable capacity is 1,150 hours, producing a baseline near 72 per hour before country-specific tax considerations.

Review the rate regularly

Compare estimated hours with actual hours, including revisions and communication. Track win rate, late payments, utilisation, and costs. Raise or restructure rates when repeated projects require more effort than the estimate supports.

Important limitations

This calculation does not determine tax, legal status, minimum rates, or market demand. Rules vary by location and profession. Use an accountant or adviser for personal tax and business-structure decisions.
