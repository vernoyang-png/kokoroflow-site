Work and money guide

Compare hourly pay and annual salary without hiding the assumptions

The right conversion depends on actual weekly hours and the number of weeks that are paid.

Reviewed: July 2026 · 5 minute read

The basic gross-pay formula

Annual gross pay = hourly rate × hours per week × paid weeks per year.

At 25 per hour, 40 hours per week, and 52 paid weeks, the simple annual estimate is 52,000. If four weeks are unpaid, using 48 paid weeks produces 48,000.

Monthly pay is not four weekly payments

A calendar year contains more than 48 weeks. For a consistent monthly average, divide annual pay by 12. For weekly budgeting, use the weekly figure; for a monthly comparison, use the annual figure divided by 12.

Gross is not take-home pay

The converter does not calculate income tax, national insurance or social contributions, pension deductions, health insurance, student loans, or other payroll items. These rules vary by country and personal circumstances. Use an official calculator or payslip for a net-pay estimate.

Compare total compensation

When comparing an hourly role with a salaried role, include paid leave, sick leave, retirement contributions, insurance, bonuses, overtime treatment, schedule stability, travel time, and equipment. A higher cash rate may not be a better total package.

Contractor rates require another calculation

A self-employed worker may pay business costs and spend substantial time on administration or marketing. Do not treat an employee hourly rate as an equivalent freelance billing rate. Use the Freelance Rate Calculator for that comparison.
