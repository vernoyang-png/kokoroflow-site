How the savings estimate works

Without investment growth

When the annual return is zero, the calculator subtracts the starting balance from the target and divides the remaining amount by the number of months. A $10,000 goal with $1,500 already saved over 24 months requires about $354.17 per month.

With an estimated return

For a nonzero return, the calculator applies a monthly compound-rate model to the starting balance and a series of end-of-month contributions. The result is a planning estimate, not a promised return. Savings-account rates can change, and investments can lose value.

Build a more resilient plan

  • Use a conservative return assumption for goals with a short deadline.
  • Keep emergency savings separate from optional goals.
  • Compare a longer timeline with a smaller monthly contribution.
  • Review the plan after income, expenses, rates, or deadlines change.

Frequently asked questions

What if current savings already exceed the target? The required contribution is shown as zero. Are taxes and fees included? No. Are deposits made at the start or end of each month? The model assumes end-of-month contributions. Is this financial advice? No; it is an educational scenario tool.

Important limitation

Real returns are rarely smooth. For a bank account, compare current annual percentage yields and withdrawal rules. For investments, consider volatility, fees, taxes, and the possibility of loss before relying on a projected balance.
