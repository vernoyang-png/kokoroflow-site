Travel planning guide

Build a trip budget that includes the easy-to-miss costs

Separate fixed costs, daily costs, optional spending, and a buffer so one change does not hide the whole plan.

Reviewed: July 2026 · 6 minute read

Start with fixed costs

List transport to the destination, accommodation bookings, visas, insurance, event tickets, and any reservation already priced. Record whether a quoted room rate includes local taxes, resort charges, cleaning fees, or breakfast.

Build a daily allowance

Estimate food, local transport, and small purchases per person per day. A daily allowance makes it easier to shorten or extend the trip without rebuilding everything. Separate shared costs, such as a hotel room, from per-person costs.

Keep optional spending visible

Activities and shopping are easier to control when they have their own limit. Mark each item as essential, preferred, or optional. If prices rise, remove optional items before reducing essentials such as insurance or safe transport.

Commonly missed costs

  • Checked luggage and seat selection.
  • Airport transfers, parking, tolls, and public-transport cards.
  • Local accommodation taxes and service charges.
  • Card, ATM, roaming, and currency-conversion fees.
  • Tips, laundry, medication, and replacement essentials.
  • Pet care or home costs while away.

Add a contingency buffer

A buffer of your choosing can absorb moderate price changes or an unplanned meal or journey. It does not replace appropriate insurance or emergency savings. Keep emergency funds separate from the normal holiday spending limit.

Review in two currencies

If travelling abroad, keep the booking currency and home currency visible. Exchange rates move, and card providers may use different rates and fees. Recheck major costs shortly before payment.
